UAE LTV (LOAN-TO-VALUE) RULES

1. Expatriates buying their first residential property:
   - Maximum LTV is 80% for properties valued up to AED 5 million.
   - This means minimum down payment required is 20%.

2. Expatriates buying second property:
   - Maximum LTV is 65%.
   - Minimum down payment required is 35%.

3. UAE Nationals:
   - Maximum LTV is 85% for properties under AED 5 million.
   - Minimum down payment required is 15%.

4. Off-Plan Property:
   - LTV is generally lower (50–60%).
   - Down payment requirements are higher (40–50%).

5. Mortgage Eligibility Requirements:
   - Minimum salary typically AED 8,000–10,000 per month.
   - Proof of employment or business ownership.
   - Clean credit history (AECB report).

6. Age Restrictions:
   - Borrower must usually not exceed age 65 (salaried) or 70 (self-employed) at maturity.

7. Non-Eligible Individuals:
   - Individuals with poor AECB credit score may be rejected.
   - Individuals with unstable income may be rejected.

CALCULATION RULE:
LTV = (Loan Amount / Property Price) * 100
Loan Amount = Property Price – Down Payment
